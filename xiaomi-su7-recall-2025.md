# Xiaomi SU7: What the 2025 Chinese Regulatory Record Establishes

**Research question:** Did Xiaomi's SU7 receive a material regulator penalty in 2025, and what happened?

**Answer:** The official sources reviewed establish a **recall, not a monetary or administrative penalty**. On January 24, 2025, China's State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR) published Xiaomi Auto Technology's recall filing for 30,931 SU7 Standard vehicles. A software-strategy timing-synchronization issue could impair intelligent parking assistance when detecting static obstacles, increasing scrape or collision risk. Xiaomi's stated remedy was a free OTA software update.

**Confidence:** High for the recall scope, risk, and remedy because each comes from the definitive regulator notice. Moderate for the narrower “no material penalty” formulation: the reviewed official record does not describe a fine or sanction, but absence from these sources is not proof that no unrelated enforcement action exists.

**Date scope:** January 1 through December 31, 2025, with a March 2026 annual summary used only for retrospective context. Research accessed July 13, 2026.

## Bilingual evidence

### Recall scope

> 召回部分SU7标准版电动汽车，共计30931台

Faithful translation: “Recall of certain SU7 Standard electric vehicles, totaling 30,931 units.”

The notice breaks that total into 18,410 BJ7000MBEVR2 vehicles, 12,117 XMA7000MBEVR2 vehicles, and 404 XMA7000MBEVR5 vehicles, produced from February 6 through November 26, 2024.

### Safety issue

> 影响智能泊车辅助功能对静态障碍物的探测

Faithful translation: “It may affect the intelligent parking-assistance function's detection of static obstacles.”

SAMR says the underlying software-strategy problem could cause abnormal timing synchronization and increase scrape or collision risk.

### Remedy

> 通过汽车远程升级（OTA）技术，为召回范围内的车辆免费升级软件

Faithful translation: “Provide a free software update to vehicles in the recall scope through over-the-air (OTA) technology.”

### Later regulatory context

> 避免企业通过OTA升级隐瞒车辆缺陷或规避责任

Faithful translation: “Prevent companies from using OTA upgrades to conceal vehicle defects or evade responsibility.”

This sentence comes from a joint MIIT–SAMR notice issued on February 25, 2025—after the Xiaomi recall. It is evidence of the broader regulatory approach, not evidence that Xiaomi concealed a defect.

## What remains uncertain

- The official recall notice does not use penalty language or state a fine. This report therefore rejects the penalty framing for this event; it does not claim an exhaustive proof that Xiaomi faced no other regulator action anywhere in 2025.
- No independent news source is needed to establish the recall's legal facts because SAMR is the definitive primary authority. Independent reporting would add narrative context, not strengthen the filing itself.
- The later OTA rules must not be projected backward as the legal basis for the January recall.
